Playable Characters

Nowe

Nowe is the main protagonist of Drakengard 2. Raised by the ancient dragon Legna, he is initially a member of the Knights of the Seal, a group dedicated to protecting the world through the use of magical Seals. However, as he witnesses the suffering caused by the Seals, he begins to question their true purpose and sets out on a journey to discover the truth. Nowe is characterized by his strong sense of justice, youthful idealism, and inner conflict as he grapples with his loyalty to the knights and his growing disillusionment with their methods.

Manah

Once a major antagonist in the original Drakengard, Manah is now seeking redemption. She played a central role in the destruction and chaos of the previous game but has since repented and aims to destroy the Seals that she once sought to control. Manah is a powerful sorceress with a tragic past marked by manipulation and trauma. Her determination to atone for her past actions and her complex relationship with Nowe drive much of the game's emotional depth.

Eris

Eris is a skilled and loyal knight of the Knights of the Seal, and a childhood friend of Nowe. She is dedicated to her duty and initially stands in opposition to Nowe when he turns against the knights. However, her unwavering loyalty to Nowe and her own sense of justice eventually lead her to join him in his quest. Eris is a strong, dependable fighter who struggles with the conflict between her duty and her personal beliefs.

Urick

Urick is a former knight who has been resurrected from death. He is a stoic and grim figure, burdened by his past and the circumstances of his resurrection. Despite his undead state, Urick remains a powerful warrior and serves as a mentor and protector to Nowe. His experience and wisdom are invaluable to the group, and his tragic backstory adds a layer of depth to his character.

Legna

Legna is an ancient dragon who has raised Nowe since he was a child. Wise and powerful, Legna serves as Nowe's guardian and mentor. In battle, Legna provides formidable aerial support and devastating attacks. His mysterious past and deep knowledge of the world's history play crucial roles in the unfolding narrative, revealing unexpected connections and truths as the story progresses.

Unplayable Characters

Gismor

Gismor is the ambitious and ruthless leader of the Knights of the Seal. He is deeply committed to maintaining the power and control of the Seals, believing it is the only way to ensure order in the world. Gismor views Nowe and his companions as threats to this order and will stop at nothing to eliminate them. His authoritarian approach and willingness to sacrifice others for his goals make him a formidable antagonist.

Seere

Seere is one of the Seals and a central figure in the game's conflict. Despite his young age, he possesses significant magical power and a tragic backstory involving the loss of his sister and his role in the events of the first Drakengard. Seere's innocence contrasts sharply with the heavy burden he carries, and his fate is deeply intertwined with the destiny of the world.

Caim

The protagonist of the original Drakengard, Caim appears as a non-playable character whose dark and violent past continues to influence the events of the sequel. Silent and driven by a deep-seated rage, Caim's actions and decisions have far-reaching consequences that affect the lives of many characters in Drakengard 2. His complex history with Angelus and his relentless pursuit of his own goals add layers of intrigue and tension to the story.

Angelus

Angelus is Caim's dragon partner from the first game, now bound as one of the Seals. Her presence is central to the game's narrative, representing both immense power and tragic sacrifice. Angelus's bond with Caim and her role in the balance of the world's magic make her a pivotal character, whose fate is closely tied to the game's climax and resolution.

Leonard

Leonard is a former knight haunted by his past sins and the loss of his loved ones. His tragic story and ultimate fate provide a poignant backdrop to the main narrative. Leonard's experiences and regrets deeply affect the motivations and actions of the main characters, adding emotional depth and complexity to their journey.

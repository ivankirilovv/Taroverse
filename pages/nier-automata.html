Release

"NieR: Automata" is an action role-playing game developed by PlatinumGames and published by Square Enix. Released in 2017 for the PlayStation 4, PC, and later for Xbox One, it is a sequel to the 2010 game NieR, itself a spin-off from the Drakengard series. Directed by Yoko Taro, the game has been acclaimed for its profound narrative, complex characters, and innovative gameplay mechanics, establishing itself as a significant title in modern gaming.

Storyline

Set in the distant future, NieR: Automata takes place on a post-apocalyptic Earth where humanity has fled to the moon following an invasion by alien-created machines. To reclaim their planet, humans deploy androids from the YoRHa organization to combat the alien machine army. The story centers on three primary android protagonists: 2B, a combat android; 9S, a scanner android; and A2, a rogue prototype.

Throughout the game, players explore themes of existentialism, consciousness, and the nature of humanity as the androids confront not only the alien machines but also their own purposes and emotions. The narrative is divided into multiple playthroughs, each revealing different perspectives and uncovering deeper layers of the story. These playthroughs are essential for understanding the full scope of the game's plot, leading to various endings that collectively deliver a powerful and thought-provoking conclusion.

Gameplay

"NieR: Automata" combines action RPG elements with fast-paced, hack-and-slash combat, characteristic of PlatinumGames' style. Players primarily control 2B, engaging in fluid and dynamic combat using a combination of melee weapons and ranged attacks. The game allows seamless switching between weapons and features a robust combo system, enabling players to perform a variety of attack combinations.

The game also includes segments where players control 9S, emphasizing hacking mechanics that shift the gameplay to a twin-stick shooter format. These hacking sequences add variety and depth to the combat experience. Additionally, the game features exploration, platforming, and bullet hell elements, creating a rich and diverse gameplay environment.

"NieR: Automata" also integrates RPG elements such as character progression, equipment upgrades, and an open world with side quests that enhance the game's lore and provide additional context to the main narrative.

Development and Reception

Directed by Yoko Taro and developed by PlatinumGames, NieR: Automata benefited from the collaboration of a studio known for its expertise in action gameplay and a director celebrated for his unconventional storytelling. The music, composed by Keiichi Okabe, received widespread acclaim for its emotional and atmospheric impact, perfectly complementing the game's themes and narrative.

Upon release, NieR: Automata was met with critical acclaim, praised for its storytelling, character development, and innovative gameplay. Critics highlighted its ability to blend different gameplay genres seamlessly and its exploration of profound philosophical themes. The game's visual design, combat mechanics, and soundtrack were also highly regarded.
